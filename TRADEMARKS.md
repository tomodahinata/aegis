## What this means

**You may**, without asking:

- use, fork, modify, and redistribute the code under MIT;
- state factually that your product "uses Aegis," "is built on Aegis," or "is compatible with Aegis";
- reference the name in truthful comparisons, reviews, articles, and talks.

**You may not**, without prior written permission:

- publish a fork, derivative, or repackaged distribution under the name "Aegis"/"AegisKit" or under the
  `@aegiskit` npm scope (**rename your fork** — pick your own name and scope);
- use the name or logo as the name of your product, company, or service, or in a way that implies
  official status, endorsement, sponsorship, or affiliation;
- register or use confusingly similar names, domains, handles, or logos.

## Why this exists

Aegis is a security tool: users must be able to trust that a package named `@aegiskit/*` is the genuine,
maintained artifact with its provenance attestation — not a look-alike that could ship a backdoor. The
trademark line is what lets the code stay fully open while the **name remains a reliable signal of
origin.** It protects users first, and the project's brand second.
